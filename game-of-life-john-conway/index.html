The Game of life

The Game of Life is not your typical computer game. It consists of a collection of cells which, based on a few mathematical rules, can live, die or multiply. Depending on the initial conditions, the cells form various patterns throughout the course of the game.

The Rules

NOTE: black box represents unpopulated cell, white for a populated cell

For a space that is 'populated':

  • Each cell with one or no neighbors dies, as if by solitude.
  • Each cell with four or more neighbors dies, as if by overpopulation.
  • Each cell with two or three neighbors survives.

For a space that is 'empty' or 'unpopulated'

  • Each cell with three neighbors becomes populated.

The initial pattern in the game is called a seed. The rules above are applied to every single cell in the seed simultanously. Births and deaths occur simultaneously. The rules continue to be applied repeatedly to create further generations
